Learning Energy Consumption Models of Manufacturing Plants

Polimi has developed a data-driven automata learning algorithm targeting Stochastic Hybrid Automata, that is, models featuring both variables with non-trivial time dynamics and stochastic processes. In a joint project with DMec, the algorithm has been successfully applied to learn a model of the energy consumption of a metalworking CNC machine. The learned model can be exploited to estimate the energy required to perform a specific sequence of operations and plan the manufacturing task from a sustainability perspective.

This proposal aims to expand the work in two directions (constituting two standalone thesis projects):

(a) Previous applications of the learning algorithm assume that differential equations constraining relevant physical variables of the system are known a priori. On the other hand, power consumption exhibits complex time dynamics whose exact model is unknown. The aim of the thesis is to find and implement the most suitable signal processing technique to identify an approximated power consumption model from available data. The work should be validated to assess the improvement in energy estimation accuracy compared to previous results.

(b) The currently learned model does not capture the probability of observing a specific sequence of operations. Therefore, the aim of this thesis is to estimate such probabilities from available data and perform post-processing of the learned model to reduce its size by pruning improbable event sequences. The refined model may then be put through planning and/or optimization techniques to identify the plan that achieves a specific goal by simultaneously minimizing energy consumption.

User-Friendly Configuration of Automata Learning Case Studies

Polimi has developed a data-driven automata learning algorithm targeting Stochastic Hybrid Automata, that is, models featuring both variables with non-trivial time dynamics and stochastic processes. The work addresses the issue of modeling complex Cyber-Physical Systems for analysis, monitoring, or software development purposes when significant sources of uncertainty---such as human behavior---are at play. The algorithm is agnostic to the specific system under learning and applicable to domains ranging from interactive robotics to smart manufacturing systems.
However, specifying the case study's features necessary to perform the learning is still time-consuming and cumbersome, even for users with good programming skills. This thesis project aims to develop a high-level user-friendly notation to specify the necessary features of the case study under analysis and a code generation procedure necessary to perform the learning in practice. The result should be validated to assess its effectiveness and the advantage compared to manual specification.

Automated Mission Re-Planning for Assistive Robotics Applications

Polimi has developed a framework for developing robotic applications in service settings (such as hospitals, home assistance, education) that can handle the unpredictability of human behavior. The framework includes a design-time analysis phase to estimate the most likely outcome of the mission (e.g., success vs. failure due to low battery charge) and, subsequently, deploy (or simulate) the application. Due to the uncertainties in the environment, the outcome observed at runtime may not match the one estimated offline, thus, requiring a reconfiguration of the mission before running new simulations.
The reconfiguration is currently performed manually by the framework's user. This thesis project aims to automate the reconfiguration (or "re-planning") phase by developing a tool to compute alternative plans for the user to choose from and automatically repeat the design-time verification.
The thesis outcome should include a solid justification for the chosen re-planning strategy and validation on significant use cases, highlighting the re-planning phase's added value.
